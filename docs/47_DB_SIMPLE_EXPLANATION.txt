===============================================================================
WHY 47 dB DIFFERENCE? - ULTRA SIMPLE EXPLANATION
===============================================================================

QUESTION: Why do the models differ by 47 dB in amplitude?

SHORT ANSWER: They use different formulas for how sound gets weaker with distance.

===============================================================================
THE TWO FORMULAS
===============================================================================

C-LINEAR MODEL (Hocine's):
--------------------------
"Sound spreads in a sphere up to 8 km, then in a cylinder beyond that"

Formula: TL = 20·log(r)  for r < 8km    (spherical)
         TL = 20·log(8km) + 10·log(r/8km)  for r > 8km  (cylindrical)

At 100 km: TL = 20·log(8000) + 10·log(100000/8000) = 78 + 11 = 89 dB


RAY PARAMETER MODEL (Atte's):
-----------------------------
"Measure how much the ray tube (bundle of neighboring rays) has expanded"

Formula: TL = -20·log( (1/4π)·√(cos(θ₀)/(c·J)) )
         where J = how much neighboring rays have spread apart

At 100 km: TL ≈ 136 dB (depends on ray focusing/defocusing)


DIFFERENCE: 136 - 89 = 47 dB ✓

===============================================================================
VISUAL ANALOGY
===============================================================================

Think of a garden hose spraying water:

C-LINEAR MODEL:
  "Water spreads evenly in all directions"

  Source:  O -----> spreading uniformly -----> weaker (but predictable)

  Loss = geometric spreading only


RAY PARAMETER MODEL:
  "Water spreads, but sometimes the hose nozzle focuses or defocuses it"

  Source:  O -----> \/\/\/ (focusing & defocusing) -----> much weaker

  Loss = geometric spreading + focusing effects


In the Munk profile, rays DEFOCUS (spread out more) → amplitude is weaker
→ Ray parameter predicts 47 dB more loss than simple model

WHICH IS RIGHT?
===============================================================================

BOTH ARE VALID PHYSICS!

C-linear:       Good for quick estimates, easy to understand
Ray parameter:  More accurate, captures wave physics better

The paper COMPARES them to show the trade-off between simplicity and accuracy.

THE MATH (if you want details)
===============================================================================

C-Linear spreading loss at 100 km:
  - Spherical 0→8 km:     20·log₁₀(8000)        = 78.06 dB
  - Cylindrical 8→100 km: 10·log₁₀(100000/8000) = 10.97 dB
  - Total:                                         89.03 dB

Ray parameter spreading loss at 100 km:
  - Jacobian formula with measured ray tube expansion
  - J ≈ very large (rays have spread out a lot)
  - Total:                                        ~136 dB

Difference: 136 - 89 = 47 dB

Add absorption (~0.1 dB, negligible at 50 Hz)
Add reflections (same for both models)

Final difference: Still 47 dB
